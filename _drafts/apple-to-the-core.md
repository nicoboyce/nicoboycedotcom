

The understated brilliance of being boring


Long time Apple fans complain about the unexciting nature of the Cook era. No innovation, no crazy experiments, no uncompromising thrusts into the future. Did they not see the goggles? Not a successful product, but unquestionably a bold and thrilling failure.

There are lots of things I hate about the iphone. I haven't had one before for my primary phone, only a couple for testing purposes. Why is there no notification log I can find? I know it's not a widely appreciated android feature but bloody hell I need this! Notifications in general are just terrible. The keyboard is rotten. The corner radius is ridiculous and it leads to some bad compromises in the UI. 



I know a lot of people have affection for the Apple inc of Steve Jobs. The products were beautiful sometimes, creative usually, wacky and valliant failures now and again. There were moments of instinctive insight, like buying the entire supply of miniature hard disks that were the core of the ipod. There were stupid things like circular mice.

I never never wanted anything Apple during the Steve Jobs time. It wasn't for me. Form over function. Opinionated interfaces which just felt needlessly contrarian to me. Fit around us, don't think you can reshape this for how you operate, their catalogue declared.

Today I have Apple everything. I don't have any loyalty or affection for the brand, but I've got shit to do and Apple is the fastest route to Stuff Getting Done.



At the same time the business quietly courted people like me. The gigging engineer who needs to show up with a machine that gets out the way. Solid performance, good battery life, won't fall to bits, no bullshit just productivity.

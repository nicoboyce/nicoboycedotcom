## Raw text

I hate that I don't love the new Forza Horizon. Alright so it looks nice. The lighting is stunning. The way wet ground is affected by wheelspin. The cars (up until S class) feel weightier, more substantial to drive. It's also a great achievement to get these environments in there, the skiing resort, the tokyo car park, things like that. It's impressive. But lots of things are impressive but I don't want them. A guess on paper a Tesla is an impressive piece of kit but I don't like being in one. The game looks incredible. I haven't been this impressed since maybe CMR04 thinking "wow look how far graphics have come" and on my Philips 4k thing with the ambilight all round it feels huge. But is this game fun? A lot of it feels like it makes no sense. What's different with treasure cars to barn finds? Why does the game throw cars at you in the first few hours? It feels so unrewarding. The voice acting is just so flat and drab. I've been playing The Crew Motorfest or something lately and that reminded me that Forza Horizon just isn't fun enough. But then we get to the racing. Bloody hell. I'm a motorsport enthusiast. I follow (and by follow I mean I watch every race though mostly on mondays and tuesdays and hope to avoid spoilers) F1, Indycar, three levels of Nascar, Imsa, RallyX, BRX, W2RC, BTCC, BHC, and there are a couple dozen other series I watch more casually like WRC, WEC, Aussie Supercars, ARCA and others. You know what literally none of these series have? A sudden little pop of a crest into a hidden dip into a mid radius 90 degree banked downhill sweeper type corner. They don't have this because everyone would crash into the outside wall every fucking time and it would be horrible. You can't judge your braking at all, your wheels are off the ground when you need to be stopping, you can't lift for the crest because you're pushed into a spin by the ai cars. But every bloody race in this game has exactly that a bunch of times. You can't turn off the rewind because you're just pitched into a spin or you nose into the wall a dozen times a race and then have to start it again (and I have other things to be doing thank you!) and so every victory feels hollow.

I just need to keep logging things I guess. The snowy area - all the trees seem to be identical and honestly that's not very good. I feel like you could have a template and variation within that? I haven't studied them but there's no life to that bit. Also the snow is just a blank flat nothing for most of it. It just feels like nothing at all. Put some animal tracks in or thinner patches or darker patches, I dunno, make it feel less empty. But then the tokyo environment. I know they need to have some urban stuff but bloody hell it's a horrible nightmare to drive around, like it presumably is in the real life. There's this twisty incomprehensible motorway junction over the daisuko parking area (is that the right spelling) which looks really accurate and precise recreation but also it's really horrible! Just concrete misery. But honestly it's the races that have really disappointed me. They are just not fun. They've added treasure cars, aftermarket cars, mascots, various things to find. The estate and garages and stuff that's like the sims. I don't want it. A game like this has to have good racing and then some tinsel and low energy bits to do in freeroam but the racing is bad and the other stuff nags at you.

I don't want to be forced to build user generated content to access things I want to do in the game but I can see why the studio decided that was a good idea. Get the players to build tracks and stuff, saves them a job. Maybe that is what will save the game though if actually good races emerge

dunno how I turn this into something anyone gives a shit about. But I was a known name in the forza horizon world such as it is for a bit. I had some world records in fh5 for a while, I did a podcast with four figures listeners, I then did reviews, tutorials, streams for gfinity and racinggames.gg and stuff so I have played them all. But I'm not really a games person. I'm a motorsport person

the map suddenly has insect noises - what are they called cicadas? It's such a nasty noise. The more I play this the angrier I get. Who thought this notoriously horrible noise would be a good addition to the map view?

yeah it's disappointing on a level only star wars can normally achieve

there are some impressive things. The reflections, even during a race on the sides of cars are so great looking. painted surfaces have more depth too - they looked a bit flat or too shiny before but now they can look like a freshly polished lexus, that's good. I'm trying to find positives

what do I compare it to for an interesting piece? F1 in 2026? That's also a joke tbf

f1 is probably more popular than star wars now though. everyone is talking about it, it's crazy. I mentioned that I'm seeing the new star wars at the weekend in a meeting earlier today and everyone was like "there's another star wars film?"

## Organised notes

### The praise (genuine)
- Lighting is stunning
- Wet ground response to wheelspin
- Cars up to S class feel weighty, substantial
- Environments are an achievement — ski resort, Tokyo car park, etc.
- On the Philips 4K with Ambilight it feels huge
- Most impressed by graphics leap since CMR04
- Reflections on car sides, even mid-race, look great
- Painted surfaces have depth now — used to look flat or too shiny; now can look like a freshly polished Lexus
- (Worth noting: "I'm trying to find positives" — the fact that the good stuff has to be hunted for is itself part of the story)

### But impressive ≠ wanted
- Tesla analogy: impressive on paper, don't like being in one
- Looking good isn't the same as being fun

### What's broken about the fun
- Treasure cars vs barn finds — what's the actual difference?
- Game throws cars at you in the first few hours — unrewarding
- Voice acting is flat and drab
- The Crew Motorfest reminded me Horizon just isn't fun enough

### The racing — the real problem
- I follow motorsport seriously: F1, IndyCar, three Nascar tiers, IMSA, RallyX, BRX, W2RC, BTCC, BHC, plus a couple dozen more casually (WRC, WEC, Supercars, ARCA…)
- None of these series feature: sudden crest → hidden dip → mid-radius 90° banked downhill sweeper
- Because real drivers would crash into the outside wall every time
- Can't judge braking, wheels airborne when you need to stop, can't lift for the crest because AI shunts you into a spin
- Every race in Horizon has several of these
- Can't turn off rewind — you'd be restarting every race a dozen times
- Result: hollow victories

### Environments — closer look
- **Snowy area**: trees all identical, no variation. Could have a template with variation within it. No life to it.
- Snow itself is a blank flat nothing for most of it. Feels empty. Wants: animal tracks, thinner patches, darker patches — anything to break it up
- **Tokyo**: they need some urban content but it's a horrible nightmare to drive around (like real life probably is)
- The twisty motorway junction over the Daisuko (sp?) parking area — accurate, precise recreation, but horrible to drive. Concrete misery.

### Sound design irritations
- The map view suddenly has cicada noises
- Notoriously horrible noise — who thought this was a good addition to a screen you sit on?
- The more I play, the angrier I get — small things like this compound

### The clutter problem
- They've piled in treasure cars, aftermarket cars, mascots, things to find
- The estate / garages / Sims-like stuff
- Don't want it
- The shape a game like this needs: **good racing core**, then tinsel and low-energy freeroam bits around it
- What we got: bad racing, and the other stuff nags at you

### User-generated content
- Don't want to be forced to build UGC to access things I want to do in the game
- But can see why the studio went that way: get the players to build tracks, saves the devs a job
- Possible silver lining: if genuinely good races emerge from the community, that might be what saves the game

### Credibility / who's writing this
- Was a known name in the Forza Horizon world for a while
- Had some world records in FH5
- Did a podcast with four-figure listeners
- Did reviews, tutorials, streams for Gfinity and racinggames.gg
- Played them all
- But: "I'm not really a games person. I'm a motorsport person."
- That last line might be the whole frame — the reason this critique exists, and the reason it lands different from a games-press review

### Possible angles / thesis seeds
- "Impressive but not wanted" as the spine
- A spectacular game that forgot racing is supposed to feel like racing
- The gap between visual achievement and design intent
- Tinsel can't carry a racing game if the racing is broken — the side stuff stops being a treat and starts being a nag
- **Motorsport person reviewing a racing game** — what a games reviewer praises as exciting track design is what a motorsport fan recognises as nonsense corners no real series would tolerate. That gap is the article.
- "Disappointing on a level only Star Wars can normally achieve" — strong candidate for an opener or pull quote. Sets the calibration: this isn't a bad game, it's a loved franchise letting you down.

### Comparison candidates (for the piece's frame)
- **F1 2026** — and it's also a joke, so the parallel works: hugely loved series, technically ambitious on paper, fundamental design choices making the actual experience worse. A motorsport-person writing about a racing game using a real-motorsport disappointment as the lens is a natural fit for your voice.
- Cultural reach has flipped: F1 is probably more current than Star Wars now. Mentioned new Star Wars film in a meeting → "there's another Star Wars film?" Meanwhile everyone's talking about F1.
- So F1 2026 isn't the obscure motorsport-nerd reference it might have been a few years ago — it's probably the more recognisable disappointment.
- Use Star Wars as a passing calibration ("only Star Wars can normally achieve this") but make F1 2026 the working parallel.

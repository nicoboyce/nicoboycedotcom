## Opening — set the calibration

> I hate that I don't love the new Forza Horizon.

> yeah it's disappointing on a level only star wars can normally achieve

> f1 is probably more popular than star wars now though. everyone is talking about it, it's crazy. I mentioned that I'm seeing the new star wars at the weekend in a meeting earlier today and everyone was like "there's another star wars film?"

## Establish who you are (the unlock)

> I was a known name in the forza horizon world such as it is for a bit. I had some world records in fh5 for a while, I did a podcast with four figures listeners, I then did reviews, tutorials, streams for gfinity and racinggames.gg and stuff so I have played them all. But I'm not really a games person. I'm a motorsport person

*

## The praise, given honestly first

> Alright so it looks nice. The lighting is stunning. The way wet ground is affected by wheelspin. The cars (up until S class) feel weightier, more substantial to drive. It's also a great achievement to get these environments in there, the skiing resort, the tokyo car park, things like that. It's impressive.

> there are some impressive things. The reflections, even during a race on the sides of cars are so great looking. painted surfaces have more depth too - they looked a bit flat or too shiny before but now they can look like a freshly polished lexus, that's good. I'm trying to find positives

> The game looks incredible. I haven't been this impressed since maybe CMR04 thinking "wow look how far graphics have come" and on my Philips 4k thing with the ambilight all round it feels huge.

*

## The pivot — impressive isn't the same as wanted

> But lots of things are impressive but I don't want them. A guess on paper a Tesla is an impressive piece of kit but I don't like being in one.

> But is this game fun?

*

## The environments, looked at closely

> The snowy area - all the trees seem to be identical and honestly that's not very good. I feel like you could have a template and variation within that? I haven't studied them but there's no life to that bit. Also the snow is just a blank flat nothing for most of it. It just feels like nothing at all. Put some animal tracks in or thinner patches or darker patches, I dunno, make it feel less empty.

> But then the tokyo environment. I know they need to have some urban stuff but bloody hell it's a horrible nightmare to drive around, like it presumably is in the real life. There's this twisty incomprehensible motorway junction over the daisuko parking area (is that the right spelling) which looks really accurate and precise recreation but also it's really horrible! Just concrete misery.

*

## The small things that compound

> the map suddenly has insect noises - what are they called cicadas? It's such a nasty noise. The more I play this the angrier I get. Who thought this notoriously horrible noise would be a good addition to the map view?

> A lot of it feels like it makes no sense. What's different with treasure cars to barn finds? Why does the game throw cars at you in the first few hours? It feels so unrewarding. The voice acting is just so flat and drab.

*

## The clutter — what you actually want versus what they gave you

> They've added treasure cars, aftermarket cars, mascots, various things to find. The estate and garages and stuff that's like the sims. I don't want it. A game like this has to have good racing and then some tinsel and low energy bits to do in freeroam but the racing is bad and the other stuff nags at you.

> I've been playing The Crew Motorfest or something lately and that reminded me that Forza Horizon just isn't fun enough.

*

## The core — the racing, with motorsport-person authority

> But then we get to the racing. Bloody hell. I'm a motorsport enthusiast. I follow (and by follow I mean I watch every race though mostly on mondays and tuesdays and hope to avoid spoilers) F1, Indycar, three levels of Nascar, Imsa, RallyX, BRX, W2RC, BTCC, BHC, and there are a couple dozen other series I watch more casually like WRC, WEC, Aussie Supercars, ARCA and others. You know what literally none of these series have? A sudden little pop of a crest into a hidden dip into a mid radius 90 degree banked downhill sweeper type corner. They don't have this because everyone would crash into the outside wall every fucking time and it would be horrible. You can't judge your braking at all, your wheels are off the ground when you need to be stopping, you can't lift for the crest because you're pushed into a spin by the ai cars. But every bloody race in this game has exactly that a bunch of times. You can't turn off the rewind because you're just pitched into a spin or you nose into the wall a dozen times a race and then have to start it again (and I have other things to be doing thank you!) and so every victory feels hollow.

*

## The UGC consolation (and skepticism)

> I don't want to be forced to build user generated content to access things I want to do in the game but I can see why the studio decided that was a good idea. Get the players to build tracks and stuff, saves them a job. Maybe that is what will save the game though if actually good races emerge

*

## Why this structure works for the format of your recent posts

Looking at "staring at your feet", "does every dog bark", "if it saves just one life":

- Section breaks with `*` separators — loose movements, not a tight argument
- Strong opening line that sets emotional pitch ("Don't ask me what I think about your pet", "If it saves just one life, isn't it worth doing? **No it fucking isn't.**")
- Authority established by lived experience, not credentials sold ("I wrote this piece outdoors", "I follow F1, Indycar, three levels of Nascar…")
- Wandering structure that loops back to the thesis
- One savage closing image or line that lands the whole piece (the cat in a sandwich; "Make risk great again")

The Forza piece has all the parts. The structural choice is: lead with the disappointment calibration (Star Wars/F1 2026), then who you are, then walk through what's wrong while doing the rare-for-you thing of granting genuine praise first. The racing rant is the centrepiece because it's where your motorsport-person authority does work no games reviewer can do. UGC is a hopeful aside before you close on the F1 2026 parallel.

What's missing for the actual piece: a closing line in your voice that's as savage as the cat-sandwich. That's not in your notes yet.

When you press start on Forza Horizon 6, the map view comes up and somebody plays you a recording of cicadas. The notoriously horrible sound. The "we are not having a nice time" noise. Who agreed to this? Who sat in a meeting and said yes, when our customer opens this menu, the thing they should hear is the world's worst insect.

*

I went to see the new Star Wars film yesterday. A Mandalorian one I think. The cinema was about a third full. Nobody clapped at the end and nobody looked annoyed either. People stood up the way you stand up in a doctor's waiting room when your name finally gets called.

When I mentioned earlier in the week in a meeting that I'd booked tickets, two people said "there's another Star Wars film?" Star Wars has lost its grip. F1 has eaten its lunch. Everyone is talking about Verstappen and nobody is talking about whoever this Mandalorian was supposed to be.

*

I hate that I don't love the new Forza Horizon. The previous five kept me company through life events I would otherwise have struggled with. I had world records in FH5 for a while. I did a podcast with four-figure listeners. I wrote reviews and tutorials and streamed for Gfinity and racinggames.gg. I was a known name in the Forza Horizon world such as it is. But I'm not really a games person. I'm a motorsport person, and a game like this is supposed to be how a motorsport person blows off steam.

The new one looks nice. Lighting is stunning. Wet ground responds to wheelspin properly. Cars up to S class feel weightier and more substantial than they used to. Reflections on the sides of cars, even mid-race, look great. Painted surfaces have depth — they used to look flat or a bit too shiny; now a freshly polished Lexus actually looks like a freshly polished Lexus. I haven't been this impressed by a graphics leap since CMR04. On my Philips with the Ambilight all round it feels huge.

A Tesla is impressive on paper. I don't want to be in one.

*

Here is the problem. I watch F1, IndyCar, three tiers of Nascar, IMSA, RallyX, BRX, W2RC, BTCC, BHC, and a couple of dozen other series more casually. WRC, WEC, Aussie Supercars, ARCA. I catch most races on a Monday or Tuesday, trying to dodge spoilers. You know what literally none of these series have? A sudden little pop of a crest into a hidden dip into a mid-radius ninety-degree banked downhill sweeper. They don't have it because everyone would crash into the outside wall every single time and it would be horrible. You can't judge your braking. Your wheels are off the ground when you need to be stopping. You can't lift for the crest because if you do, the AI puts you into a spin.

Every race in this game has several of these. You cannot turn the rewind off because if you do you're restarting every event a dozen times and I have other things I would like to do this year. So every victory is hollow. You did not win. You used the magic button.

*

The snowy area is a flat blank nothing. The trees are all the same tree. Put some variation in the template, lads. The snow is a sheet — give it animal tracks, thinner patches, darker patches, anything to make it feel like somewhere. Tokyo, fine, they had to have an urban environment, but there is a twisty incomprehensible motorway junction over the Daisuko parking area which is a precise and accurate recreation of a horrible piece of concrete misery. Top work, I suppose.

Cicadas on the map. Voice acting flat and drab. There are treasure cars and barn finds, which as far as I can tell are the same thing under two different labels. The game throws cars at you in the first few hours so nothing you receive feels earned. There is an estate, and garages, and Sims-like decoration, and mascots to find, and aftermarket parts, and none of it would matter if the racing was good. The racing is bad and the side stuff nags at you like a child in a supermarket.

You can build your own tracks, too. They would like you to do that. They'd like you to do quite a lot of the work, in fact.

*

I've been playing The Crew Motorfest for a few weeks and it reminded me Horizon isn't fun enough. Which got me wondering, what is this. Why do they make a sequel every couple of years if the studio doesn't have anything to say. Why does F1 2026 — also a joke — exist at all.

It is the same shape as the Mandalorian film. Same shape as a dozen other things I could name. Not bad. Not good. Within the window of acceptable outcomes.

That's the defining feature of this period. Planned mediocrity. The product is going to perform exactly as planned. A good return on the investment. No runaway success. No unexpected supply chain issues with merch. No need to rush the follow-up. The people who liked the previous one will buy this one. They will not be too excited and they will not be too disappointed, and that is the beginning and the end of the ambition. Money in. Modest, predictable money out. No surprises in either direction.

It is not that FH6 is a bad game and it is not that the new Star Wars is a bad film. They are exactly the game and the film they were always going to be. They were built to hit a number. They hit it.

*

Microsoft owns Forza. Disney owns Star Wars. When the meetings happened where these were greenlit, everyone in the room knew what they were going to get. They got it. Everyone got paid. Nobody got fired. The shareholders nodded. The next one is already on the schedule.

I want things made by people who might lose their jobs if it goes wrong. I want films and games that could have been better and could have been worse and instead turned out to be either, sharply, in a way nobody planned for. Bring back the swing. Bring back the embarrassment. Bring back the meeting where somebody says "you spent how much?" and somebody else has to answer.

Make risk great again. They can have my Xbox when they pry it from my cold, dead hands.
